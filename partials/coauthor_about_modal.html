Co-authorship Network Visualization

This interactive visualization shows the collaboration network based on co-authorship relations extracted from the bibtex file. It helps identify clusters of authors that collaborate frequently; to detect those authors that serve as gatekeepers by interconnecting different clusters; understand the backbone of strong co-author linkages that likely serve as main collaboration and knowledge diffusion pathways.
  • Each node represents an author.
  • Two authors are connected if they have authored a paper together.
  • Fill color denotes the year of first publication.
  • Node outline color indicates author type (student, faculty, post-doc, staff, other)
  • Node size and label size denote the number of papers authored.
  • Edge thickness denotes the number of times two authors appeared on a paper together.
  • The horizontal bar graph on the right shows the number of papers per author.
  • The legend details how the information is being encoded.
